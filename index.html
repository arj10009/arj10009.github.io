About

This is my corner of the internet — a lab notebook and a public trail of thinking. I believe reflection creates leverage, and leaving a record keeps me honest. If you're here, you're watching me build in real time.

I trained in two worlds: mathematics taught me to think in systems — discrete structures, operations research, the elegance of clean logic. Medicine taught me people — uncertainty, empathy, the mess of real life. The combination shaped how I approach everything.

Right now I'm a junior doctor in the WA public system, deliberately scaling back clinical hours to create space for building. The goal: healthcare technology that actually matters. Spatial computing. AI tools. Workflows that don't suck. I don't have it all figured out — I'm building the capacity to figure it out.
